## Hölder smoothness consistency (Option B)

### Summary

Resolved inconsistency between main text (which assumes $\eta_0 \in H^\beta([0,1]^d)$ for $\beta > 0$ and $\beta > d/2$) and appendix (which defined $H^\beta$ only for $\beta \in (0,1]$). Implemented Option B: general Hölder space for $\beta > 0$ and extended Lemma 1 proof.

### Key changes

- **paper/manuscript.tex**
  - **Notation (Lemma 1 proof):** Replaced the paragraph that defined $H^\beta$ only for $\beta \in (0,1]$ with the standard definition for $\beta > 0$: $\ell = \lfloor \beta \rfloor$, $\gamma = \beta - \ell \in (0,1]$; all partial derivatives of order $0, \ldots, \ell$ bounded; $D^\alpha \eta$ $\gamma$-Hölder for $|\alpha| = \ell$. Noted that for $\beta \in (0,1]$ this reduces to the usual Hölder condition.
  - **Lemma 1 proof:** Added a “Case $\beta > 1$” paragraph after the generic-loss paragraph: piecewise-constant approximation on a regular partition achieves $L^2$ error $O(s^{-\beta/d})$ (squared $O(s^{-2\beta/d})$), with brief justification via Taylor and citations to DeVore \& Lorentz and Györfi et al.; concluded $\inf_{f \in \cT_s} [R(f) - R(\eta_0)] \le C s^{-2\beta/d}$ via the loss--norm link.
- Main text (conditions, $\beta > 0$, $\beta > d/2$) and Lemma 1 statement, optimal $s_n$ lemma, and nuisance-rate theorem left unchanged.

## Flesh out Rashomon proofs (plan)

### Summary

Implemented the “flesh out Rashomon proofs” plan so the theory is defensible and checkable before finalizing the API contract (Implementation requirements doc) for the other package. All edits in **paper/manuscript.tex**; no plan file edits.

### Key changes

1. **General theorem (DML with alternative nuisances)**
   - **Definition (def:close):** Specified that “close” means componentwise $L^2(P)$: $\|\tilde{e}^{(-k)} - e_0\|_{L^2(P)}$, $\|\tilde{m}_0^{(-k)} - m_{0,0}\|_{L^2(P)}$, $\|\tilde{m}_1^{(-k)} - m_{1,0}\|_{L^2(P)}$ each $o_p(n^{-1/4})$. Noted that under overlap these are the relevant norms for the ATT score.
   - **Theorem (thm:dml-alt):** Added explicit “$K$ is fixed.” Proof now gives (i) explicit remainder bound (constant × products of nuisance $L^2$ errors, with ref to overlap-weighted norms), (ii) precise reference to \citet{chernozhukov2018}, Section 3.2 and Assumption 3.2, (iii) statement that the only nuisance-dependent condition is remainder $o_p(n^{-1/2})$.

2. **Proposition (Rashomon selection and DML validity)**
   - **New Lemma (lem:oracle-near):** Oracle inequality for *near-minimizers*: any $f \in R_k(\varepsilon_n)$ with $\#\text{leaves}(f) \le s_n$ (or $O_p(s_n)$) satisfies $R(f) - R(\eta_0) \le C(\varepsilon_n \cdot \text{best}^{(-k)} + s_n \log n/n + \inf_{f' \in \cT_{s_n}}[R(f') - R(\eta_0)]) + o_p(1)$, with short proof idea.
   - **Proposition:** Stated sufficient condition $\varepsilon_n \to 0$ and $\varepsilon_n = o(n^{-1/2})$. Proof uses Lemma lem:oracle-near and the loss–norm link: excess risk $o_p(n^{-1/2})$ ⇒ $\|f - \eta_0\|_{L^2(P)} = o_p(n^{-1/4})$ (squared loss).

3. **Definition (def:intersection)**  
   - Clarified **refit**: minimize $R_n^{(-k)}(f) + \lambda_n \cdot (\#\text{leaves of } f)$ over all trees $f$ with that same structure (same splits, variable leaf values).
