\subsubsection{Exact structure recovery under a structural margin}
\label{app:margin}

The stability assumption (A4) of Theorem~\ref{thm:intersection-existence} is an
\emph{existence} statement: it posits a single structure that is
$(1+\varepsilon_n)$-near-optimal on every fold, but says nothing about
\emph{why} such a structure should exist as $\varepsilon_n\to 0$. This leaves
the method exposed to a genuine tension, which we call the \emph{Rashomon
catch-22}:
\begin{itemize}
\item valid double-machine-learning inference requires
  $\varepsilon_n = o(n^{-1/2})$ (Corollary~\ref{cor:intersection-overhead},
  Proposition~\ref{prop:rashomon-dml}: the intersection overhead is
  $O_p(\varepsilon_n\cdot\text{best}^{(-k)})$ with $\text{best}^{(-k)}=O_p(1)$
  by Lemma~\ref{lem:best-bounded}, and $\sqrt n\,\varepsilon_n = o(1)$ is
  needed);
\item but as $\varepsilon_n\to 0$ the per-fold Rashomon sets
  $R_k(\varepsilon_n)$ shrink toward the fold-specific minimizers, whose
  \emph{optimal structures} may differ across folds, so
  $\bigcap_{k}R_k(\varepsilon_n)$ can be empty
  (Remark~\ref{rem:intersection-empty}).
\end{itemize}
Enlarging $\varepsilon_n$ post hoc to force a non-empty intersection re-violates
$\varepsilon_n = o(n^{-1/2})$ and is a data-adaptive, post-selection device that
no theorem here covers. Assumption~\ref{assump:margin} below dissolves the
tension by upgrading the existence statement (A4) to a \emph{separation}
statement about the population-optimal structure.

\begin{assumption}[Structural margin]\label{assump:margin}
Work over the sieve $\cT_{M_n}$ of trees with at most $M_n$ leaves on $d$ binary
features, under the setting of Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle}
($\eta_0\in[c,1-c]$, clipped leaves; or the squared-error analogue). For a tree
\emph{structure} (partition) $\tau$, write its population penalized risk as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pop-pen}
  P(\tau) \;:=\; \min_{\text{leaf values}}
     \bigl\{\, R(f_\tau) + \lambda_n\cdot\#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau)\,\bigr\},
  \qquad R(f) = \E[L(Z,f(X))],
\end{equation}
and its fold-$k$ empirical analogue
\begin{equation}\label{eq:emp-pen}
  P_n^{(-k)}(\tau)
  \;:=\; \min_{\text{leaf values}}
     \bigl\{\, R_n^{(-k)}(f_\tau) + \lambda_n\cdot\#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau)\,\bigr\},
  \qquad
  \text{best}^{(-k)} = \min_{\tau:\ \#\mathrm{leaves}\le M_n} P_n^{(-k)}(\tau).
\end{equation}
Let $\tau^\ast := \argmin_{\tau:\ \#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau)\le M_n} P(\tau)$ and
assume this minimizer is \emph{unique}, with
$\#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau^\ast)\le L_n\asymp
n^{s/(2\alphabar+s)}$ as in Theorem~\ref{thm:nuisance-rate}. There exists a
sequence $\Delta_n>0$ such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:margin}
  G_\tau\;:=\;P(\tau) - P(\tau^\ast)\;\ge\;\Delta_n
  \qquad\text{for every structure } \tau\neq\tau^\ast
  \text{ with } \#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau)\le M_n,
\end{equation}
and the margin dominates the estimation noise floor:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:margin-rate}
  \frac{\Delta_n}{\, L_n\log(nd)/n\,}\;\longrightarrow\;\infty
  \qquad\text{equivalently}\qquad
  \Delta_n \;\gg\; \frac{L_n\log(nd)}{n}.
\end{equation}
\end{assumption}

\noindent\textbf{Relation to (A4).}
Assumption~\ref{assump:margin} strictly strengthens the existence-only condition
(A4) of Theorem~\ref{thm:intersection-existence}. (A4) asks that \emph{some}
structure be near-optimal on every fold; \eqref{eq:margin} asks that the
\emph{population} optimum be strictly separated from \emph{all} competitors by a
gap $G_\tau$ that outgrows the per-structure estimation error. It rules out the
near-ties that Remark~\ref{rem:intersection-empty}(1)--(4) identify as the
sources of an empty intersection. Uniqueness of $\tau^\ast$ is part of the
assumption; if the argmin is not unique, \eqref{eq:margin} fails with
$\Delta_n\equiv 0$ and the conclusions below do not apply. Note that the margin
condition binds only through the \emph{small} competitors: for any structure
$\tau$ with more leaves than $\tau^\ast$, the penalty term
$\lambda_n(\#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau)-\#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau^\ast))>0$ in
\eqref{eq:pop-pen} \emph{adds} to $G_\tau$, so such competitors only make the
margin larger; the binding competitors are the low-complexity ones, and
\eqref{eq:margin} is a statement about them (made quantitative in the two-stratum
argument of Step~3). This restricts the substantive content of (A5) to a finite,
low-complexity neighborhood of $\tau^\ast$ and makes it more plausible.

\begin{lemma}[Local curvature and recentered refit oracle inequality]\label{lem:local-curvature}
Adopt the setting of Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle} (cross-entropy,
$\eta_0\in[c,1-c]$, clipped leaves; or squared error with $Y\in[0,1]$). Fix a
structure $\sigma$ with $\ell:=\#\mathrm{leaves}(\sigma)$ cells $A_1,\dots,A_\ell$
that each carry positive mass under both $P$ and $P_n^{(-k)}$. Let $g_\sigma$ be
the population refit and $f_\sigma^{(k)}$ the fold-$k$ empirical refit of $\sigma$
(leaf values minimizing $R$ and $R_n^{(-k)}$ respectively over the fixed
partition $\sigma$), and let $\cT_\sigma\subset\cT_\ell$ be the (at most)
$\ell$-parameter class of trees on $\sigma$ with values in $[c,1-c]$. Write
$\psi_f=\ell(\cdot,f)-\ell(\cdot,\eta_0)$ and, for the recentered increments,
$\chi_f:=\psi_f-\psi_{g_\sigma}=\ell(\cdot,f)-\ell(\cdot,g_\sigma)$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(i)] \emph{(Strong convexity of the population refit.)} The map
  $v\mapsto R(f_{\sigma,v})$, where $f_{\sigma,v}$ is the tree on $\sigma$ with
  leaf-value vector $v\in[c,1-c]^{\ell}$, is $m_c$-strongly convex in the
  $L_2(P)$ geometry, with
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:curv-const}
    m_c \;=\;
    \begin{cases}
      \dfrac{1}{(1-c)^{2}}\ \ (\ge 1), & \text{cross-entropy},\\[4pt]
      2, & \text{squared error},
    \end{cases}
  \end{equation}
  and consequently, for every $f\in\cT_\sigma$,
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:sc-lower}
    R(f) - R(g_\sigma)\;\ge\;\tfrac{m_c}{2}\,\norm{f-g_\sigma}_{L_2(P)}^2 .
  \end{equation}
\item[(ii)] \emph{(Lipschitz increment link.)} For any $f\in\cT_\sigma$,
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:var-link}
    \norm{\chi_f}_\infty\le V_c,\qquad
    P\chi_f^2\;\le\; \kappa_c^2\,\norm{f-g_\sigma}_{L_2(P)}^2
      \;=\; 2B_c\,\norm{f-g_\sigma}_{L_2(P)}^2,
  \end{equation}
  with $V_c=\log\tfrac{1-c}{c}$, $\kappa_c=\tfrac{1}{c(1-c)}$,
  $B_c=\tfrac{\kappa_c^2}{2}=\tfrac{1}{2c^2(1-c)^2}$, by the sup-norm and
  Lipschitz increment of Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle}, Step~1.
\item[(iii)] \emph{(Recentered variance proxy.)} Composing (i)--(ii),
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:recentered-var}
    P\chi_f^2\;\le\; \frac{4B_c}{m_c}\,\bigl[R(f)-R(g_\sigma)\bigr]
    \qquad\text{for all } f\in\cT_\sigma;
  \end{equation}
  i.e. the recentered loss class $\{\chi_f:f\in\cT_\sigma\}$ has a
  \emph{$g_\sigma$-localized} variance proxy linear in the excess risk over
  $g_\sigma$, with envelope $V_c$.
\item[(iv)] \emph{(Recentered refit oracle inequality.)} On the sub-root
  fixed-point event of Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle}, Step~3, applied to the class
  $\{\chi_f:f\in\cT_\sigma\}$ with the variance proxy \eqref{eq:recentered-var}
  and envelope $V_c$, with probability $\ge 1-e^{-u}$,
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:refit-excess}
    R\bigl(f_\sigma^{(k)}\bigr) - R(g_\sigma)
    \;\le\; C_3\,(B_c+V_c)\,\frac{J_\ell}{n}
    \;=:\; \rho_n(\ell),
    \qquad J_\ell=C\ell\log(nd),
  \end{equation}
  and, on the same event, the recentered fluctuation modulus obeys
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:diff-modulus}
    \sup_{\substack{f\in\cT_\sigma:\\ \|f-g_\sigma\|_{L_2(P)}^2\le\varrho}}
       \bigl|(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\chi_f\bigr|
    \;\le\; c_2\!\left(\sqrt{\frac{2B_c\,\varrho\,J_\ell}{n}}
        + \frac{(B_c+V_c)J_\ell}{n}\right),
    \qquad \varrho>0.
  \end{equation}
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(i) Curvature.} For cross-entropy, $R(f_{\sigma,v})$ decomposes over the
disjoint cells as $R(f_{\sigma,v})=\sum_{j=1}^\ell P(A_j)\,\E[\ell(Y,v_j)\mid
X\in A_j]$. Writing $a_j=\E[\eta_0(X)\mid X\in A_j]\in[c,1-c]$, the per-cell risk
is $\phi(v_j):=-a_j\log v_j-(1-a_j)\log(1-v_j)$, with
$\phi''(v)=a_j/v^2+(1-a_j)/(1-v)^2\ge a_j(1-c)^{-2}+(1-a_j)(1-c)^{-2}=(1-c)^{-2}$
on $[c,1-c]$. Summing weighted by $P(A_j)$ and using
$\norm{f_{\sigma,v}-f_{\sigma,v'}}_{L_2(P)}^2=\sum_j P(A_j)(v_j-v_j')^2$ gives
$m_c$-strong convexity with $m_c=(1-c)^{-2}\ge1$; a second-order Taylor expansion
at the interior minimizer $g_\sigma$ yields \eqref{eq:sc-lower}. For squared
error the per-cell second derivative is $2$, so $m_c=2$.
\emph{(ii) Increment link.} By Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle}, Step~1(a),
$\norm{\psi_f}_\infty\le V_c$; the difference of two such increments over the
common baseline $\eta_0$ is bounded by the loss oscillation, so
$\norm{\chi_f}_\infty\le V_c$. By Step~1(b),
$|\chi_f|=|\ell(\cdot,f)-\ell(\cdot,g_\sigma)|\le\kappa_c|f-g_\sigma|$ pointwise,
whence $P\chi_f^2\le\kappa_c^2\norm{f-g_\sigma}_2^2=2B_c\norm{f-g_\sigma}_2^2$,
the boxed \eqref{eq:var-link} (for squared error $\chi_f$ is $2$-Lipschitz on
$[0,1]$, same form).
\emph{(iii) Composition.} From \eqref{eq:sc-lower},
$\norm{f-g_\sigma}_2^2\le\tfrac{2}{m_c}[R(f)-R(g_\sigma)]$; substituting into the
variance bound $P\chi_f^2\le 2B_c\norm{f-g_\sigma}_2^2$ gives
$P\chi_f^2\le\tfrac{4B_c}{m_c}[R(f)-R(g_\sigma)]$, the boxed
\eqref{eq:recentered-var}.
\emph{(iv) Recentered oracle.} Run the Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle} Step~2--4
machinery \emph{recentered at $g_\sigma$} on $\{\chi_f:f\in\cT_\sigma\}$. Bousquet's
three inputs are supplied by (ii)--(iii): envelope $\norm{\chi_f}_\infty\le V_c$;
$g_\sigma$-localized variance proxy $P\chi_f^2\le\tfrac{4B_c}{m_c}[R(f)-R(g_\sigma)]$;
entropy of $\{\chi_f:f\in\cT_\sigma\}$ bounded by that of $\cT_\ell$,
$J_\ell=C\ell\log(nd)$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:complexity}; $f\mapsto\chi_f$ is
$\kappa_c$-Lipschitz). Symmetrization + Dudley give the sub-root envelope; Bousquet
(Bousquet 2002; Massart 2000) at localized variance $\le 2B_c\varrho$ (via
\eqref{eq:var-link}) gives the modulus \eqref{eq:diff-modulus} at radius-squared
$\varrho$; the Bartlett--Bousquet--Mendelson fixed point $r^\star_\ell\le
C''(B_c+V_c)J_\ell/n$ and peeling convert to population excess, giving
\eqref{eq:refit-excess}. Because $g_\sigma$ is the population minimizer over
$\cT_\sigma$, no approximation term appears: the fixed point is the whole bound.
This is a genuine recentered oracle inequality, not a call to the $\eta_0$-centered
parent.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Exact structure recovery and non-empty intersection]
\label{thm:margin-recovery}
Assume the setting and hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle} (so the
Step-3 machinery and Lemma~\ref{lem:local-curvature} hold), $K$ fixed,
and Assumption~\ref{assump:margin}. Then, with probability tending to one as
$n\to\infty$, for every fold $k=1,\dots,K$ simultaneously the fold-$k$ empirical
penalized-risk minimizing structure is exactly $\tau^\ast$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:exact-recovery}
  \argmin_{\tau:\ \#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau)\le M_n} P_n^{(-k)}(\tau)
  \;=\; \tau^\ast,
  \qquad k=1,\dots,K.
\end{equation}
Consequently, for \emph{any} $\varepsilon_n\ge 0$ (in particular for
$\varepsilon_n\asymp \log(n)/n = o(n^{-1/2})$),
\begin{equation}\label{eq:nonempty}
  \tau^\ast \;\in\; \bigcap_{k=1}^{K} R_k(\varepsilon_n),
  \qquad\text{so}\qquad
  \bigcap_{k=1}^{K} R_k(\varepsilon_n)\neq\varnothing
  \quad\text{w.p.}\to 1.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\emph{Proof strategy.} We show the fold-$k$ empirical penalized risk of every
competitor structure exceeds that of $\tau^\ast$. We split competitors at a
threshold $\kappa L_n$ ($\kappa$ a fixed constant, Step~3): \emph{small}
competitors ($\le\kappa L_n$ leaves), for which we bound the fluctuation
\emph{competitor-adaptively} against each competitor's own population gap
$G_\tau$; and \emph{large} competitors ($>\kappa L_n$ leaves), dominated by the
penalty gap. Convergence is \emph{in probability} (events of probability $\to1$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Step 1 (empirical/population decomposition).}
Fix a fold $k$ and a competitor $\tau\neq\tau^\ast$, $L(\tau):=\#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau)
\le M_n$. Let $f_\tau^{(k)},g_\tau$ be the empirical/population refits of $\tau$.
The penalty cancels in the gap:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gap-decomp}
  P_n^{(-k)}(\tau) - P_n^{(-k)}(\tau^\ast)
  \;=\; \underbrace{\bigl[P(\tau)-P(\tau^\ast)\bigr]}_{=\,G_\tau\ \ge\ \Delta_n\ \text{by }
        \eqref{eq:margin}}
     \;+\; \underbrace{\bigl[\Delta_n^{(k)}(\tau)-\Delta_n^{(k)}(\tau^\ast)\bigr]}_{
        \text{fluctuation}},
\end{equation}
where for each $\sigma\in\{\tau,\tau^\ast\}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fluct-def}
  \Delta_n^{(k)}(\sigma)
  \;:=\; P_n^{(-k)}(\sigma) - P(\sigma)
  \;=\; R_n^{(-k)}(f_\sigma^{(k)}) - R(g_\sigma)
  \qquad(\text{penalty }\lambda_n L(\sigma)\text{ cancels}).
\end{equation}

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Step 2 (three-term recentered split, keeping the
$g_\sigma$-average).} Add and subtract $R(f_\sigma^{(k)})$ and write
$R_n^{(-k)}(f_\sigma^{(k)})-R(f_\sigma^{(k)})=(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{f_\sigma^{(k)}}$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fluct-split-raw}
  \Delta_n^{(k)}(\sigma)
  \;=\; \bigl[R(f_\sigma^{(k)})-R(g_\sigma)\bigr]
     \;+\; (P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{f_\sigma^{(k)}}.
\end{equation}
Splitting the increment through $g_\sigma$ via
$\psi_{f_\sigma^{(k)}}=\chi_{f_\sigma^{(k)}}+\psi_{g_\sigma}$ (with
$\chi=\psi_{f_\sigma^{(k)}}-\psi_{g_\sigma}$) and \emph{retaining} the
fixed-function average $(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{g_\sigma}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:fluct-recentered}
  \bigl|\Delta_n^{(k)}(\sigma)\bigr|
  \;\le\; \underbrace{\bigl[R(f_\sigma^{(k)})-R(g_\sigma)\bigr]}_{\text{refit-excess}}
   \;+\; \underbrace{\bigl|(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\chi_{f_\sigma^{(k)}}\bigr|}_{\text{recentered modulus}}
   \;+\; \underbrace{\bigl|(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{g_\sigma}\bigr|}_{\text{fixed-function average}}.
\end{equation}
The first two terms are $g_\sigma$-local (small); the third is a single fixed
function whose variance scales with $\sigma$'s \emph{approximation} error and is
handled competitor-adaptively below. \emph{Retaining this third term is the
correction:} dropping it would falsely claim a uniform $\rho_n$ floor, whereas
for a coarse competitor $\tau$ (few leaves, $\Theta(1)$ approximation error)
$|(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{g_\tau}|$ is of order $n^{-1/2}\gg\rho_n$, and must be
compared against $\tau$'s own gap $G_\tau$, not against $\rho_n$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Step 3 (two-stratum, competitor-adaptive control).}

\emph{Choice of boundary.} Let $\Delta^\ast_n:=R(g_{\tau^\ast})-R(\eta_0)$. By
\emph{penalized}-optimality of $\tau^\ast$ (it minimizes $P(\cdot)$, so
$R(g_{\tau^\ast})+\lambda_n L(\tau^\ast)\le R(g_{\tau_{L_n}})+\lambda_n L_n$ for
the $L_n$-leaf approximating structure $\tau_{L_n}$ of Lemma~\ref{lem:approx}),
\begin{equation}\label{eq:tau-star-approx}
  \Delta^\ast_n
  \;\le\; \bigl[R(g_{\tau_{L_n}})-R(\eta_0)\bigr] + \lambda_n L_n
  \;\le\; C_{\mathrm{ap}}\,L_n^{-2\alphabar/s} + \lambda_n L_n
  \;\le\; C_5\,\rho_n,
\end{equation}
using $L_n^{-2\alphabar/s}\asymp\lambda_n L_n\asymp\rho_n$ (balance defining
$L_n$, Theorem~\ref{thm:nuisance-rate}), with $\rho_n=C\lambda_n L_n$. Fix
$\kappa:=1+2C_5C$.

\emph{Population-gap bookkeeping for a competitor.} For any competitor
$\tau$, decompose its approximation error through $\tau^\ast$:
\begin{align}
  R(g_\tau)-R(\eta_0)
  &= \bigl[R(g_\tau)-R(g_{\tau^\ast})\bigr] + \Delta^\ast_n \nonumber\\
  &\le \bigl[P(\tau)-P(\tau^\ast)\bigr] + \lambda_n\bigl(L(\tau^\ast)-L(\tau)\bigr)
      + \Delta^\ast_n \nonumber\\
  &\le G_\tau + \lambda_n L(\tau^\ast) + \Delta^\ast_n
  \;\le\; G_\tau + C_6\rho_n,
  \label{eq:gtau-approx}
\end{align}
using $R(g_\tau)-R(g_{\tau^\ast})=[P(\tau)-\lambda_n L(\tau)]-[P(\tau^\ast)-
\lambda_n L(\tau^\ast)]=G_\tau+\lambda_n(L(\tau^\ast)-L(\tau))$, dropping the
nonpositive penalty term $\lambda_n(L(\tau^\ast)-L(\tau))$ when $L(\tau)\ge
L(\tau^\ast)$ (and bounding it by $\lambda_n L(\tau^\ast)\le\lambda_n L_n
\asymp\rho_n$ otherwise), and $\Delta^\ast_n\le C_5\rho_n$; $C_6$ absorbs both.

\emph{Stratum (i): small competitors, $L(\tau)\le\kappa L_n$.} Bound each term of
\eqref{eq:fluct-recentered} for $\sigma\in\{\tau,\tau^\ast\}$.
(a) \emph{Refit-excess:} $\le\rho_n(L(\sigma))\le\rho_n(\kappa L_n)=:\rho_n'
\asymp\rho_n$ by Lemma~\ref{lem:local-curvature}(iv).
(b) \emph{Recentered modulus:} by (iv),
$\norm{f_\sigma^{(k)}-g_\sigma}_2^2\le\tfrac{2}{m_c}\rho_n'$, so the modulus
\eqref{eq:diff-modulus} at $\varrho=\tfrac{2}{m_c}\rho_n'$ and $J_{\kappa L_n}$ is
$\le c_2(\sqrt{2B_c\cdot\tfrac{2}{m_c}\rho_n'\cdot J_{\kappa L_n}/n}+(B_c+V_c)
J_{\kappa L_n}/n)\le C_7'(B_c+V_c)(1+\sqrt{B_c/m_c})J_{\kappa L_n}/n\asymp\rho_n$,
since $\rho_n'\asymp J_{\kappa L_n}/n$ so $\sqrt{\rho_n' J_{\kappa L_n}/n}\asymp
J_{\kappa L_n}/n$.
(c) \emph{Fixed-function average $(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{g_\sigma}$:} $\psi_{g_\sigma}$
is a single bounded function with $\norm{\psi_{g_\sigma}}_\infty\le V_c$ and, by
Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle} Step~1(b) (variance link at $\eta_0$),
$P\psi_{g_\sigma}^2\le B_c\,[R(g_\sigma)-R(\eta_0)]$. Bernstein's inequality for a
single bounded function (van der Vaart--Wellner 1996; equivalently Bousquet with
a one-point class) gives, with probability $\ge1-e^{-u}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bernstein-g}
  \bigl|(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{g_\sigma}\bigr|
  \;\le\; \sqrt{\frac{2\,B_c\,[R(g_\sigma)-R(\eta_0)]\,u}{n}}
        + \frac{V_c\,u}{3n}.
\end{equation}
Take a union bound over the $\le\kappa L_n$-leaf structures with exponent
$u=C_1(\kappa L_n)\log(2d)+\log K+v=O(L_n\log(nd))+\log K+v$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:complexity}); so $u\asymp J_{\kappa L_n}\asymp\rho_n\cdot n$, and
$V_c u/(3n)\asymp\rho_n$. For $\sigma=\tau^\ast$, $R(g_{\tau^\ast})-R(\eta_0)=
\Delta^\ast_n\le C_5\rho_n$, so \eqref{eq:bernstein-g} gives
$|(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{g_{\tau^\ast}}|\le\sqrt{2B_c C_5\rho_n\cdot u/n}+
O(\rho_n)\asymp\sqrt{\rho_n\cdot\rho_n}+O(\rho_n)=O(\rho_n)$ (using $u/n\asymp
\rho_n$). For $\sigma=\tau$, by \eqref{eq:gtau-approx} the variance is
$\le B_c(G_\tau+C_6\rho_n)$, so
\begin{equation}\label{eq:g-tau-bound}
  \bigl|(P_n^{(-k)}-P)\psi_{g_\tau}\bigr|
  \;\le\; \sqrt{\frac{2B_c\,(G_\tau+C_6\rho_n)\,u}{n}} + \frac{V_c u}{3n}
  \;\le\; C_8\Bigl(\sqrt{B_c\,G_\tau\,\rho_n} + \rho_n\Bigr),
\end{equation}
where the last step uses $u/n\asymp\rho_n$ and $\sqrt{(G_\tau+\rho_n)\rho_n}
\le\sqrt{G_\tau\rho_n}+\rho_n$. Collecting (a)--(c) into
\eqref{eq:fluct-recentered} for both $\sigma$'s and substituting into
\eqref{eq:gap-decomp},
\begin{equation}\label{eq:small-dev}
  \bigl|\Delta_n^{(k)}(\tau)-\Delta_n^{(k)}(\tau^\ast)\bigr|
  \;\le\; C_9\,\rho_n \;+\; C_8\sqrt{B_c\,G_\tau\,\rho_n}
  \qquad\text{on }\mathcal{E}_n,\ \text{uniformly over small }\tau,k,
\end{equation}
where $C_9$ absorbs the refit-excess, recentered-modulus, and $\tau^\ast$-average
pieces (all $O(\rho_n)$) and $\mathcal{E}_n$ (probability $\ge1-e^{-v}\to1$) is
the intersection of the recentered fixed-point event of
Lemma~\ref{lem:local-curvature}(iv) and the Bernstein event
\eqref{eq:bernstein-g}, both union-bounded over the small stratum and $K$ folds.

\emph{Stratum (ii): large competitors, $L(\tau)>\kappa L_n$.} From
\eqref{eq:pop-pen} and $R(g_\tau)\ge R(\eta_0)$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:large-margin}
  G_\tau \;\ge\; -\Delta^\ast_n+\lambda_n\bigl(L(\tau)-L(\tau^\ast)\bigr).
\end{equation}
Since $L(\tau)-L(\tau^\ast)>\kappa L_n-L_n=2C_5C\,L_n$ and $\Delta^\ast_n\le
C_5\rho_n=C_5C\lambda_n L_n\le\tfrac12\lambda_n(L(\tau)-L(\tau^\ast))$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:large-dom}
  G_\tau
  \;\ge\; \lambda_n\bigl(L(\tau)-L(\tau^\ast)\bigr)-\Delta^\ast_n
  \;\ge\; \tfrac12\,\lambda_n\bigl(L(\tau)-L(\tau^\ast)\bigr)
  \;>\; \tfrac12\,\lambda_n\,C_5C\,L_n \;>\;0,
\end{equation}
with an \emph{explicit} $\tfrac12$. The fold-$k$ fluctuation of any refit over
$\cT_{L(\tau)}$ is $O_p(\sqrt{L(\tau)\log(nd)/n})=o(\lambda_n L(\tau))$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle}, Step~4, part of the parent oracle event), since
$\lambda_n L(\tau)/\sqrt{L(\tau)\log(nd)/n}=\sqrt{L(\tau)\log(nd)/n}\to\infty$ for
$L(\tau)>\kappa L_n\to\infty$. Hence
$P_n^{(-k)}(\tau)-P_n^{(-k)}(\tau^\ast)\ge\tfrac12\lambda_n(\text{surplus})
-o_p(\lambda_n\,\text{surplus})>0$ on $\mathcal{E}_n$, uniformly, with no
structure-count union charged (the penalty margin grows without bound).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Step 4 (margin beats the small-stratum fluctuation).}
For a small competitor $\tau$ with $G_\tau\ge\Delta_n\gg\rho_n$
\eqref{eq:margin-rate}, we show the right side of \eqref{eq:small-dev} is
$<G_\tau$. The linear piece: $C_9\rho_n=o(\Delta_n)\le o(G_\tau)$, so
$C_9\rho_n\le\tfrac14 G_\tau$ for $n\ge n_1$. The cross piece: by AM--GM
$\sqrt{ab}\le\tfrac{\epsilon}{2}a+\tfrac{1}{2\epsilon}b$ with $a=G_\tau$,
$b=B_c\rho_n$, $\epsilon=\tfrac12/C_8$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cross}
  C_8\sqrt{B_c\,G_\tau\,\rho_n}
  \;\le\; \tfrac14 G_\tau + C_8^2\,B_c\,\rho_n
  \;\le\; \tfrac14 G_\tau + \tfrac14 G_\tau
  \;=\; \tfrac12 G_\tau,
\end{equation}
where $C_8^2 B_c\rho_n\le\tfrac14 G_\tau$ holds because $G_\tau\ge\Delta_n\gg
\rho_n$ (so $\rho_n=o(G_\tau)$, and $B_c=O(1)$ at fixed overlap) for $n\ge n_2$.
Adding, $|\Delta_n^{(k)}(\tau)-\Delta_n^{(k)}(\tau^\ast)|\le\tfrac14 G_\tau+
\tfrac12 G_\tau=\tfrac34 G_\tau$ for all $n\ge\max(n_1,n_2)$ on $\mathcal{E}_n$,
uniformly over small $\tau$. (Recentering plus the own-gap comparison removed the
$\sqrt{\Theta(1)\cdot\rho_n}=\sqrt{\rho_n}$ inflation: the surviving cross term is
$\sqrt{G_\tau\rho_n}$, dominated by $G_\tau$ precisely because $G_\tau\gg\rho_n$,
so the honest requirement is the sharp $\Delta_n\gg\rho_n$.)

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Step 5 (strict separation and exact recovery).}
For small competitors, \eqref{eq:gap-decomp} with $G_\tau\ge\Delta_n$ and Step~4,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:strict}
  P_n^{(-k)}(\tau) - P_n^{(-k)}(\tau^\ast)
  \;\ge\; G_\tau - \tfrac34 G_\tau
  \;=\; \tfrac14 G_\tau \;\ge\; \tfrac14\Delta_n \;>\; 0;
\end{equation}
for large competitors, Step~3(ii) gives the same conclusion directly. Thus
$P_n^{(-k)}(\tau^\ast)<P_n^{(-k)}(\tau)$ for every $\tau\neq\tau^\ast$ on every
fold, i.e. \eqref{eq:exact-recovery}; since $\Pr(\mathcal{E}_n)\to1$, w.p.\ $\to1$.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Step 6 (non-empty intersection).}
On the recovery event, for each $k$ the refit of $\tau^\ast$ attains
$P_n^{(-k)}(\tau^\ast)=\text{best}^{(-k)}$, so for any $\varepsilon_n\ge0$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:in-Rk}
  R_n^{(-k)}(f_{\tau^\ast}^{(k)}) + \lambda_n\,\#\mathrm{leaves}(\tau^\ast)
  = P_n^{(-k)}(\tau^\ast) = \text{best}^{(-k)}
  \le (1+\varepsilon_n)\,\text{best}^{(-k)},
\end{equation}
giving $\tau^\ast\in R_k(\varepsilon_n)$ (Definition~\ref{def:rashomon-fold}) for
every $k$; by Definition~\ref{def:intersection},
$\tau^\ast\in\bigcap_{k=1}^K R_k(\varepsilon_n)\neq\varnothing$
\eqref{eq:nonempty}, at $\varepsilon_n=0$ a fortiori at
$\varepsilon_n\asymp\log(n)/n=o(n^{-1/2})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Rashomon tolerance without the intersection trade-off]
\label{cor:margin-resolution}
Under the hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:margin-recovery}, and additionally
assuming that each leaf of $\tau^\ast$ carries positive empirical mass on every
fold (\emph{positive-leaf-mass}: $\#\{i\in\mathcal{D}^{(-k)}:X_i\in A_j\}\ge1$ for
every leaf $A_j$ of $\tau^\ast$ and every $k$; holds w.p.\ $\to1$ for the fixed
structure $\tau^\ast$ with $O(L_n)$ leaves, since each leaf has positive
population mass and $n_{-k}\to\infty$), take $\varepsilon_n\asymp \log(n)/n$.
Then, with probability tending to one:
\begin{enumerate}
\item[(a)] \emph{(Non-empty intersection.)}
  $\bigcap_{k=1}^K R_k(\varepsilon_n)\neq\varnothing$, containing $\tau^\ast$,
  with no data-adaptive inflation of $\varepsilon_n$.
\item[(b)] \emph{(Zero intersection overhead.)} The refit
  $\tilde{\eta}^{(-k)}$ of the selected intersection structure $\tau^\ast$ on
  fold $k$ \emph{coincides with} the fold-$k$ penalized minimizer
  $\hat\eta^{(-k)}$, so the overhead of
  Corollary~\ref{cor:intersection-overhead} is exactly zero:
  \[
    R(\tilde\eta^{(-k)}) - R(\hat\eta^{(-k)}) = 0,
    \qquad k=1,\dots,K,
  \]
  strictly sharper than the $O_p(\varepsilon_n)$ bound of
  Corollary~\ref{cor:intersection-overhead}.
\item[(c)] \emph{(Valid inference, no conflict.)} Since
  $\varepsilon_n\asymp\log(n)/n = o(n^{-1/2})$, Proposition~\ref{prop:rashomon-dml}
  applies verbatim: $\tilde\eta^{(-k)}$ is $o_p(n^{-1/4})$-close to $\eta_0$ and,
  by Theorem~\ref{thm:dml-alt},
  \[
    \sqrt{n}\,(\hat\theta - \theta_0)\;\leadsto\; N(0,\sigma^2),
    \qquad \sigma^2 = \Var\bigl[\psi(O;\theta_0,\eta_0)\bigr].
  \]
\end{enumerate}
Thus the catch-22 dissolves: a single tolerance $\varepsilon_n\asymp\log(n)/n$
simultaneously yields a non-empty intersection and the $o(n^{-1/2})$ validity
rate, with no post-selection auto-tuning of $\varepsilon_n$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
\emph{(a)} is \eqref{eq:nonempty} at $\varepsilon_n\asymp\log(n)/n$. \emph{(b)}:
on the recovery event \eqref{eq:exact-recovery}, $\tau^\ast$ is the unique
fold-$k$ minimizing structure (strict inequality \eqref{eq:strict}), so its refit
$\tilde\eta^{(-k)}=f_{\tau^\ast}^{(k)}$ attains
$P_n^{(-k)}(\tau^\ast)=\text{best}^{(-k)}$, i.e. it equals $\hat\eta^{(-k)}$: both
minimize $R_n^{(-k)}(f)+\lambda_n\#\mathrm{leaves}(f)$ over $\cT_{M_n}$; the
minimizing structure is unique on the event, and given that structure the
leaf-value refit is the unique minimizer of a strictly convex objective (strictly
convex by Lemma~\ref{lem:local-curvature}(i), $m_c>0$) \emph{provided each leaf
has positive empirical mass} (the positive-leaf-mass condition; without it a leaf
value is unidentified and the refit non-unique). Hence
$R(\tilde\eta^{(-k)})-R(\hat\eta^{(-k)})=0$; the $O_p(\varepsilon_n)$ term in
Corollary~\ref{cor:intersection-overhead} is identically $0$. \emph{(c)}: with
$\varepsilon_n\asymp\log(n)/n$, $\sqrt n\,\varepsilon_n\to0$, so
$\varepsilon_n=o(n^{-1/2})$; Proposition~\ref{prop:rashomon-dml}'s sufficient
condition holds, and its conclusion (via Theorem~\ref{thm:dml-alt}) gives the
CLT. No step enlarges $\varepsilon_n$ using the data.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Scope and honesty of Assumption~\ref{assump:margin}]
\label{rem:margin-scope}
\emph{When (A5) is plausible.} The structural margin holds when the
population-optimal partition is well separated from all competitors --- the
generic situation for \emph{discretely structured} signal: a nuisance genuinely
piecewise-constant (or nearly so) on a dominant active subcube, with the relevant
splits carrying non-vanishing risk reductions. The binary-feature DGPs
(DGP1--DGP3) with a dominant active subcube are of this type: one partition
strictly dominates, $\Delta_n$ is bounded away from $0$ (indeed $\Delta_n\asymp
1\gg L_n\log(nd)/n$), and \eqref{eq:margin-rate} holds comfortably. The two-stratum
structure also clarifies plausibility: only \emph{small} competitors ($\le\kappa
L_n$ leaves) can threaten $\tau^\ast$; (A5) is a statement about a finite,
low-complexity neighborhood. Theorem~\ref{thm:margin-recovery} thus predicts the
$100\%$ intersection non-emptiness observed empirically
(Remark~\ref{rem:intersection-empty}) \emph{and} explains it.
\emph{When (A5) fails.} The margin degenerates ($\Delta_n\to0$, possibly
$\Delta_n\ll L_n\log(nd)/n$) when competing partitions are near-tied: rough or
high-dimensional nuisances requiring fold-specific splits, smoothly varying
(non-piecewise-constant) $\eta_0$ where many partitions approximate equally well,
or weak-overlap regimes (DGP4) where extreme propensity weights inflate the
effective variance and blur the population ranking of structures. There the
small-stratum fluctuation \eqref{eq:small-dev} need not be $<G_\tau$: recovery
\eqref{eq:exact-recovery} can fail, the intersection can be empty, and the
correct fallback is fold-specific trees (Remark~\ref{rem:intersection-empty};
\texttt{use\_rashomon = TRUE}). Data-adaptive enlargement of $\varepsilon_n$ is
\emph{not} covered here; it is a post-selection device outside the theory, voiding
the $o(n^{-1/2})$ guarantee of Corollary~\ref{cor:margin-resolution}(c). Finally,
non-emptiness is necessary but not sufficient: even under (A5), DGP4-type weak
overlap can degrade finite-sample coverage through propensity weights,
independently of structure recovery (Remark~\ref{rem:intersection-empty}).
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Post-proof audit of Theorem~\ref{thm:margin-recovery} and
Corollary~\ref{cor:margin-resolution}]
\label{rem:margin-audit}
\emph{Assumption check.} All hypotheses are declared before the statements:
overlap $\eta_0\in[c,1-c]$ and clipping (from Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle}, used
for $V_c,B_c,\kappa_c,m_c$ in Lemma~\ref{lem:local-curvature} and Steps~3--4);
binary features and $\le M_n$ leaves (via Lemma~\ref{lem:complexity}, small-stratum
union in Step~3); $K$ fixed (finite fold union); approximation balance
(Theorem~\ref{thm:nuisance-rate}/Lemma~\ref{lem:approx}) and \emph{penalized}
optimality of $\tau^\ast$ (\eqref{eq:tau-star-approx}, \eqref{eq:gtau-approx}, and
$\kappa$); (A5)---uniqueness and \eqref{eq:margin}--\eqref{eq:margin-rate}
(Steps~4--5); positive-leaf-mass for Corollary~(b). No assumption is introduced
mid-proof. Lemma~\ref{lem:hats} is \emph{not} used anywhere and is not invoked;
the small-stratum range is controlled by the explicit $\kappa L_n$ threshold, and
Corollary~(b) identifies $\hat\eta^{(-k)}$ with the refit of $\tau^\ast$ directly
from \eqref{eq:exact-recovery}.
\emph{Dependency check.} Reused: Theorem~\ref{thm:ce-oracle} Step~1 (sup-norm
$V_c$, Lipschitz $\kappa_c$, variance link $B_c$) and Steps~2--4, re-run
\emph{recentered at $g_\sigma$} in Lemma~\ref{lem:local-curvature}(iv)
(Bousquet/Massart with the $g_\sigma$-localized variance proxy
\eqref{eq:recentered-var}, Dudley/symmetrization van der Vaart--Wellner, BBM
fixed point) --- conditions (envelope $V_c$, variance $\tfrac{4B_c}{m_c}r$)
verified in (ii)--(iii); Bernstein for a single bounded function $\psi_{g_\sigma}$
(van der Vaart--Wellner; envelope $V_c$, variance $\le B_c[R(g_\sigma)-R(\eta_0)]$)
in \eqref{eq:bernstein-g}; Lemma~\ref{lem:loss-norm} (excess $\ge0$; curvature
$g''$); Lemma~\ref{lem:approx} + penalized optimality (\eqref{eq:tau-star-approx},
\eqref{eq:gtau-approx}); Lemma~\ref{lem:complexity} (finite count, small stratum);
Lemma~\ref{lem:best-bounded} (catch-22 framing);
Proposition~\ref{prop:rashomon-dml}, Theorem~\ref{thm:dml-alt} (CLT) with
$\varepsilon_n=o(n^{-1/2})$ verified. No circularity: recovery is from the
recentered oracle deviation, the single-function Bernstein bound, and the
deterministic penalty gap --- not from non-emptiness.
\emph{Rate verification.} Noise floor $\rho_n=CL_n\log(nd)/n$. The small-stratum
fluctuation splits into $O(\rho_n)$ pieces (refit-excess, recentered modulus at
$g_\sigma$-local radius $\varrho\asymp\rho_n/m_c$ so its cross term is
$\asymp J_{\kappa L_n}/n\asymp\rho_n$, and the $\tau^\ast$-average via
$\Delta^\ast_n\le C_5\rho_n$) \emph{plus} the competitor-adaptive term
$\sqrt{B_c G_\tau\rho_n}$ from the retained $g_\tau$-average
\eqref{eq:g-tau-bound}. Crucially this last term scales with the competitor's
\emph{own} gap $G_\tau$, so AM--GM \eqref{eq:cross} gives $\le\tfrac12 G_\tau$
exactly when $G_\tau\ge\Delta_n\gg\rho_n$; there is no uniform $\sqrt{\rho_n}$
floor, and the honest requirement is the sharp $\Delta_n\gg\rho_n$. Union
$C_1\kappa L_n\log(2d)=O(\rho_n n)$ absorbed into $J_{\kappa L_n}$ and into the
Bernstein exponent $u$; large stratum \eqref{eq:large-dom} charged nothing; fold
union $+\log K$. Validity uses $\sqrt n\varepsilon_n\to0$.
\emph{Edge-case scan.} (i) $\Delta_n\to0$ with $\Delta_n\lesssim\rho_n$: Step~4
$\sqrt{G_\tau\rho_n}\not\le\tfrac12 G_\tau$, separation fails --- near-tie regime.
(ii) \emph{Coarse competitor} (few leaves, $\Theta(1)$ approximation error): the
retained $g_\tau$-average has variance $\Theta(1)/n$, i.e.
$|(P_n-P)\psi_{g_\tau}|=O_p(n^{-1/2})\gg\rho_n$; this is \emph{not} compared to
$\rho_n$ but to $G_\tau$ via \eqref{eq:g-tau-bound}--\eqref{eq:cross}, and since a
coarse competitor with $\Theta(1)$ approximation error has $G_\tau=\Theta(1)\gg
n^{-1/2}$, it is comfortably beaten --- the defect of a uniform $\rho_n$ floor is
resolved by the own-gap comparison. (iii) Weak overlap $c\to0$: $B_c\sim1/(2c^2)$,
$V_c\sim\log(1/c)$ grow, $m_c=(1-c)^{-2}\to1$ (curvature does not help), inflating
$D_n$ and the cross constant $C_8^2 B_c$; \eqref{eq:margin-rate} then demands a
larger $\Delta_n$ (consistent with DGP4). (iv) Non-unique $\tau^\ast$:
$\Delta_n=0$, vacuous by design. (v) Empty leaf of $\tau^\ast$: violates
positive-leaf-mass; Corollary~(b) fails --- flagged. (vi) Heavy tails: bounded
losses assumed, out of scope.
\emph{Tightness.} The achieved recovery rate is the sharp $\Delta_n\gg\rho_n$
(recentering + own-gap comparison, no $\sqrt{\rho_n}$ inflation). Sharp \emph{up
to the boundary} $\Delta_n\asymp\rho_n$: there the empirical ranking of the two
closest structures is a coin flip, so no argument recovers the exact structure
w.p.\ $\to1$; a matching Le~Cam two-point lower bound is left to future work.
Zero-overhead (b) is exact on the recovery event.
\emph{Failure localization.} The fragile step is Step~3--4: the competitor-adaptive
comparison rests on (a) the recentered curvature $m_c$ of
Lemma~\ref{lem:local-curvature} controlling the $g_\sigma$-local radius, and (b)
the population-gap bookkeeping \eqref{eq:gtau-approx} bounding the retained
$g_\tau$-average's variance by $B_c(G_\tau+\rho_n)$. If $m_c$ degenerates or the
approximation balance $\Delta^\ast_n\asymp\rho_n$ fails, the $g_\sigma$-local
radius or the boundary constant $\kappa$ cannot be fixed and separation weakens.
The resolving device is a quantitative lower bound on $\Delta_n$ in terms of the
smallest split-level risk reduction of $\tau^\ast$ and the curvature $m_c$; we
leave this and the matching lower bound to future work.
\end{remark}
